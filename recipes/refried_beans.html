Refried Beans

Description

Refried beans are a traditional dish made from cooked beans—most commonly pinto or black beans—that are mashed and cooked again with fat, oil, or seasonings. Despite the name, they are usually not fried twice; the term comes from a translation of the Spanish frijoles refritos, meaning “well-fried beans.”

They have a smooth, creamy texture and a rich, savory flavor, making them a popular staple in Mexican and Tex-Mex cuisine. Refried beans are often served as a side dish or used as a filling for burritos, tacos, tostadas, and other dishes.

Chickpea sandwich filling is a plant-based spread made by mashing cooked chickpeas and mixing them with ingredients such as mayonnaise, yogurt, mustard, lemon juice, herbs, and finely chopped vegetables. Ingredients List

  • 1 pound dry pinto beans, rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons minced garlic, divided
  • 1 medium tomato, diced
  • 2 tablespoons ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • salt to taste

Steps

  1. Place beans in a large saucepan and cover with water by 1 inch. Place over high heat and bring to a boil. When beans come to a boil, drain and return them to the same pot.
  2. Cover beans with water by 2 inches; stir in 1 tablespoon garlic, tomato, cumin, and chili powder. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce heat to low, and simmer until beans are very soft, about 3 hours and 45 minutes, adding water as needed.
  3. Once beans have cooked, mash them with remaining garlic, oil, and salt; add water as needed to achieve desired consistency. Place over low heat for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
